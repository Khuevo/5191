The University of North Texas provides students with access to Blackboard. Blackboard is an e-learning software that allows instructors to publish

content, communicate with students, and perform other actions to manage their courses.

Students may access Blackboard to view important course information like content, grades, messages, and due dates for assignments.

 

Many classes place content on the Blackboard; however, the amount of content will vary by professor. Some courses rely heavily on the site, using

it to administer quizzes and tests, facilitate class discussions, communicate important announcements, and provide feedback on graded assignments.

Many online courses are also conducted primarily through this program.

 

Features

Blackboard offers many different features to students.

Students may access the below features through their home page:

  • Courses
  • Organizations
  • Academic Support
  • UNT Helpdesk
  • UNT Library

 

Access

The University of North Texas’s Blackboard portal can be accessed here www.learn.unt.edu.

To access this service, students must sign in to the page using their Enterprise-Wide User-ID (EUID, which is tied to each UNT student) and its associated password.

They can also choose to access the service via MyUNT or log in directly to the page.
